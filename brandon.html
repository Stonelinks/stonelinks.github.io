Micriophone robot

Hey Brandon! Here is how I’d go about making a microphone robot. While not exhaustive, this is more of a general technical overview to what my approach would be.

Step 0: Define some goals

Essentially what we’re looking to do here is remotely position a microphone in a precise location in 3d space relative to an amplifier.

Remote operation:

At best, we should be able to operate this thing from the internet. At worst, maybe it is controlled from a really long USB cable going upstairs to the control room.

Hold a microphone:

This will be the payload of the robot. The robot should be able to carry a microphone that weighs at most 300 grams. You could probably mount the microphone by just using a large black binder clip or something.

Position things in 3d space:

What this means is that given a user input of some X Y Z coordinates, the robot should move the microphone to that exact spot.

Step 1: Do some research

It turns out the general problem we’re trying to solve here is actually a pretty common. Plotters, CNC machines, gantry systems, industrial robot arms and even teleoperated cranes are all solving some form of “how do we get a machine to precisely position something”. We’re probably the first ones to apply it to audio production. Luckily this is something I have a lot of experience with.
